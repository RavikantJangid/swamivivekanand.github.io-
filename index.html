🌼 Birth & Introduction

Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda was one of India's greatest spiritual leaders, philosophers, and social reformers. He inspired millions of people through his teachings on spirituality, self-confidence, humanity, and national pride.

He was born on 12 January 1863 in Kolkata (formerly Calcutta), West Bengal, India. His birth name was Narendranath Datta. His father, Vishwanath Datta, was a successful lawyer, and his mother, Bhuvaneshwari Devi, was a deeply religious and kind-hearted woman.

From childhood, Narendranath was highly intelligent, fearless, curious, and interested in meditation and spirituality. Later, he became the chief disciple of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, who guided him toward spiritual realization.

Swami Vivekananda became world famous after his historic speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago on 11 September 1893, where he began with the famous words, "Sisters and Brothers of America."

His life continues to inspire people across the world with the message of strength, unity, service to humanity, and spiritual awakening.
